1. Flowers can be grown by multiple nurseries
2. Nurseries can grow many flowers
3. Distributors can purchase from multiple nurseries
4. Nurseries can sell to many distributors
5. Retailers can purchase from one distributor

Info:

Distributors set a standard price markup percentage for each flower they 
distribute.

For example, Distributor A sets a markup of 10% on flowers. If they 
purchase a flower from any nursery for $5.00, their price to retailers 
would be $5.50.

Retailers also set a standard price markup percentage for each flower 
they sell to consumers.

For example, Retailer A sets a markup of 15% on flowers. If they 
purchase a flower from any distributor for $3.00, their price to 
consumers would be $3.45.
